Transformations are Darwinian.

We help clients evolve systematically. Using 1.5 million datapoints from 5,000+ organizations, Trianz has developed a Digital Enterprise Evolution Model (DEEM) to help clients understand the patterns of digital evolution, prioritize correctly, and execute successfully to achieve stakeholder impact and rejuvenate their businesses.Pioneers in digital transformation are ahead because they were the first to take risks, experiment, invest ahead and iterate several times before getting the transformation process right. IT Agility and Optimization Through the Evolution

As digitalization accelerates onward, infrastructure becomes a dynamic, complex, and critical part of the transformation game. There are three important facets of infrastructure management to consider during this digital shift.Benefits of cloud at scale: While companies in most industries are still figuring out their cloud strategy, high tech is seeing massive reduction in Infrastructure costs and acceleration of solution delivery due to its enterprise-wide adoption of cloud.First, the sheer volume and scope of change in applications and data landscapes create new demands of scale and management. Second, migrations to the cloud – which in reality will take several years for large organizations – create a need to manage both cloud and on-prem environments. And finally, the way infrastructure itself is managed is changing with the availability of new tools, automation, and artificial intelligence (AI).

Benchmarking: The Foundation for Your Digital Transformation Strategy

Benchmarking should be the first step on your digital transformation journey. It will provide you with the insights needed to determine the digital gap between your organization and your competitors. It will also help you develop your vision and strategic priorities to catch up or leap ahead of your competition. We’ve collected data on digital transformations from business and IT leaders at more than 5,000 companies across all revenue segments and 20 industries. This research has given us an understanding of how “digital champions” – what we call the 7% of companies that have been successful with digital transformation – have evolved to where they are today. Using a data-driven approach, we help companies understand where they are on the digital transformation spectrum, per our Digital Enterprise Evolution Model.Along with digitalization, business models and processes are changing from a traditional way of doing things to a new paradigm that requires new data inputs: “post-digital” key performance indicators (KPI), or new KPI.
